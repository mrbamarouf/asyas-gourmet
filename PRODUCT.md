# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Guests opening Asya's Gourmet from a QR code on mobile while deciding what to order. They need quick scanning, clear prices, category filtering, warm food imagery, and direct contact links without visual clutter.

## Product Purpose

Present the Asya's Gourmet Turkish menu as a premium, warm, gourmet browsing experience that feels like a polished restaurant menu, not a generic generated list. Success means the logo remains untouched, the menu is easy to browse on mobile, and guests can quickly move from discovery to WhatsApp, Instagram, location, or the Foost source menu.

## Brand Personality

Warm, refined, generous. The tone should feel like Turkish hospitality with quiet luxury: deep navy, cream, warm beige, muted gold, charcoal, and food photography used with restraint.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic Lovable landing-page structure, fake cart behavior, overdecorated animations, AI-looking generated logo variants, crowded hero sections, tiny unreadable menu cards, or RTL layout shifts that change the page structure when switching language.

## Design Principles

- Let the original logo lead the identity without redrawing, recoloring, cropping, or filtering it.
- Build mobile first for QR scanning, with sticky search and category access close to the thumb.
- Keep the menu practical: price, image, name, and description must be clear before decorative storytelling.
- Use local approved assets for visual consistency and keep remote Foost imagery as data reference only.
- Language switching changes content, not layout geometry.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target readable contrast, large tap targets, reduced-motion support, no layout-breaking RTL direction changes, and clear link labels for contact actions.
